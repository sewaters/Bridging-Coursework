During my second year of studying BSc Computer Science, I studied a range of different modules some of which to further my knowledge I had developed in first year and others to allow me to learn about areas I had not yet looked into. In my first Semester I studied the modules; Systems Programming in C and C++, Software Engineering and Advanced Functional Programming. Then in my second semester I studied; Security and Networks, Mathematical Modelling and Decision Making and finally Team Project. My favourite module to study was Security and Networks, however I developed many different team working skills through the coursework I completed in software engineering and team project which are highly valuable to me when I graduate from my degree.

Software Engineering

During my first semester I completed group coursework as a group of 7 in the module ‘Software Engineering’ which was my first large team project. As a team we created and designed our own app to help improve the wellbeing of student aged adults. As we were learning the different stages and diagrams in a creation stage of a piece of software the coursework allowed us to put this into practice applying it for our own design. This really helped me consolidate the learning process by seeing real life examples that I had to work through myself. As I was part of a team, we developed our time management skills as well as the ability to divide up tasks and work in smaller groups too. As a team we had at least one weekly meeting as a whole group and smaller meetings throughout the week. We kept a weekly plan to make sure all tasks were being completed and everyone knew their role.


Team Project

These skills were highly useful when it came to my team project group coursework in second semester. As a group of 6 we designed and created a 2D top-down car racing game. We included many elements such as; multi-player across different laptops, networking, database, graphics, audio and physics. As the game was very integrated we had to make sure we knew what each other was doing and how our parts fitted in. Due to Covid-19 we also finished the team project remotely, audio calling and using GitHub to piece the work together for the final product. It was a great opportunity to work closely on code linked to others work and develop my java skills to the next level in a varied way.



Security and Networks

I had not studied this area before this year. I had the opportunity to learn about websites, file access and cryptography. Learning both how to protect technology and how some would take advantage of technology showed how in all aspects of development of any software it needs to be thought about. I found these topics very interesting and some of the methods very clever. I could see how this topic affects every day lives of all people and how integrated it has to be.
